3D printing is a way of making 3-dimensional objects from a design created on a computer. 3D printing is an additive manufacturing process which means that some material, usually filament, resin, or powder, is added in layers to form an object. An additive manufacturing process is a process that adds material to create an object, whereas a subtractive manufacturing process cuts away material to create an object.


A 3-D printer takes a long string of plastic called filament, pushes it through a heater, and then out a nozzle, and lays it in layers on the heated bed, or build platform. Motors, called stepper motors, move the build platform and the nozzle back-and-forth to make these layers. Then, the nozzle moves up to make the next layer. The LCD screen controls the 3-D printer.
